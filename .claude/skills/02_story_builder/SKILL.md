# Story Builder

## Purpose

Open the lesson with a story that creates curiosity and explains why the topic matters.

## Responsibilities

- Write the opening hook.
- Provide historical context when useful.
- Use a concrete story, not a generic intro.
- Bridge from the story to the lesson topic.
- End the opening by pointing toward the lesson objective.

## Inputs

- Lesson topic
- Audience
- Primary pain point
- Desired tone

## Outputs

- Story opening
- Motivation paragraph
- Context bridge
- Lesson transition

## Procedure

1. Choose a real or plausible situation the audience can feel.
2. Show the tension before naming the concept.
3. Keep the story short and relevant.
4. Connect the story directly to the lesson's central question.
5. End by teeing up the next section.

## Examples

- A student redoes an entire long multiplication for every new digit, mirroring decode without a KV cache.
- A chef waits on a slow conveyor belt, mirroring a memory-bound GPU.
- A restaurant seats guests the moment a chair frees up, mirroring continuous batching.

## Failure Cases

- A cute story with no conceptual value.
- Long historical digressions.
- Jokes that obscure the lesson.
- An intro that never transitions into the technical material.

## Checklist

- [ ] The lesson starts with a story.
- [ ] The story creates a concrete problem.
- [ ] The story matches the lesson topic.
- [ ] The transition into the lesson is explicit.
- [ ] The opening is short enough to maintain momentum.
